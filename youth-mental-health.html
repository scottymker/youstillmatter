Youth Mental Health Toolkit

Students and young people thrive when their well-being is centered. This toolkit equips educators, caregivers, and youth leaders with ready-to-use supports that nurture resilience and open conversations.

Who is this for? Middle & high school classrooms • Student clubs • Counselors • Parent & caregiver groups

Classroom-ready resources

Lesson sparks

  • “Feelings vocabulary” warm-up with emotion cards or digital polls.
  • Resilience timeline activity for students to map past wins.
  • Mindful movement break guide for 5-minute resets.

Printable supports

  • Weekly mood tracker with check-in prompts.
  • Self-care menu poster highlighting low-cost ideas.
  • Peer support agreement template for student leaders.

Student voice spotlight

Invite students to share perspectives and shape the supports they want to see. Their insights are vital for building a culture of care.

Conversation starters

  • “What helps you feel safe speaking up in class?”
  • “How can adults show they are listening without judgment?”
  • “What is one change that would make school feel more supportive?”

Leadership ideas

  • Host monthly student-led wellness circles.
  • Create a peer ambassador program for new students.
  • Collaborate with art or music classes for mental health awareness campaigns.

Family & caregiver connections

Tools for home

  • Weekly check-in questions to prompt open conversations.
  • Family calming corner checklist with sensory-friendly items.
  • Guide to co-creating a support plan with your teen.

Community partners

  • Local youth crisis centers and after-school programs.
  • Parent support groups through NAMI or YMCA chapters.
  • School-based health clinics with mental health providers.

Emergency & ongoing support

If a young person is in immediate danger, contact emergency services. Use the resources below for crisis and ongoing mental health support.

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (US) Call or text 988 for round-the-clock support.
  • National Text Line Text HOME to 741741 for free crisis counseling.
  • Teen Line (US) Call (800) 852-8336 or text TEEN to 839863 every evening.
  • Kids Help Phone (Canada) Call 1-800-668-6868 or text CONNECT to 686868.
  • Childline (UK & Ireland) Call 0800 1111 or chat online at childline.org.uk.
