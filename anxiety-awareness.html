Anxiety Awareness Hub

Anxiety can feel overwhelming, but you are not alone. We curated evidence-informed guidance, grounding practices, and community resources to support your mind and body in moments of stress.

Need support right now? Text HOME to 741741 (US/Canada) or message Shout at 85258 (UK) for immediate text-based help.

Understand what you are feeling

Common signs of anxiety

  • Racing thoughts, difficulty focusing, or feeling on-edge.
  • Muscle tension, headaches, or stomach discomfort.
  • Rapid heartbeat, shortness of breath, or lightheadedness.

When to seek extra support

  • Anxiety interferes with work, school, or relationships.
  • You avoid people, places, or experiences you usually enjoy.
  • You have thoughts of self-harm or feel unsafe. Reach out to a professional immediately.

Grounding exercises you can try today

Five senses reset

  • Notice 5 things you can see around you.
  • List 4 things you can touch and describe how they feel.
  • Identify 3 things you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste.

Regulate with breath and body

  • Box breathing: inhale, hold, exhale, hold for four counts each.
  • Progressive muscle relaxation: tense and release each muscle group from toes to forehead.
  • Self-compassion pause: place a hand over your heart and say, “This is hard, and I’m doing my best.”

Create your personal coping plan

Download, print, or screenshot this plan to keep supportive strategies close at hand. Update it when you discover new tools that work for you.

My calm toolkit

  • Three people I can text or call when I need reassurance.
  • Two grounding activities that help me reset.
  • One mantra or affirmation that reminds me I am safe.

My professional support

  • Therapist or counselor contact information.
  • Doctor or psychiatrist details for medication questions.
  • Emergency contacts and crisis lines that serve my area.
